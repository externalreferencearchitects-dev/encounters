The installation01

Built from what
the city discards.

An ephemeral architectural installation built from urban organic waste transformed into biomaterials. A shelter to rest, play, and reflect — a moment of stillness within the festival.

The shelter transforms a fragment of the festival into a living room for the commons: a soft landscape where architecture is not an object to admire, but an experience lived through the body and the senses. Inside the pavilion, artworks and collaborations with artists create moments of encounter and reflection.

Design proposals02

Three visions,
one circular language.

We propose three architectural directions for the shelter — each a distinct spatial and material interpretation of the same circular principles.

Nest

Central gathering beneath a woven bioplastic scale skin, grounded in hexagonal shell flooring.

Breath

A solar-responsive lattice of panels that open and close with the sun — a structure that breathes with the day.

Stillness

A low-sensory refuge beneath a canopy of hanging biomaterial spheres — a quiet pod within the noise.

The groundNest foundation

Hexagonal tiles from
shells.

Eggshell + Mussel Composite

Ground mussel shells and calcium-rich eggshells cast into hexagonal tiles. This modular flooring system creates flexible seating zones without requiring adhesives or mechanical fasteners.

After the festival, all tiles return to composting. The hexagonal geometry echoes natural tessellations found in honeycombs and flower petals — biomimetic design as foundational principle.

A scaffold grid wrapped in translucent panels of varying density and opacity. Darker, denser panels face the strongest sun for shading; lighter, transparent panels let daylight through — a facade that breathes in response to solar exposure.

Inside the pavilionArt & Collaboration

Space for artistic
encounters.

To foster engagement and purpose in the project through artistic interventions. To communicate the values of the pavilion and the festival through art, generating an experience of elevation, reflection, and respect for the space and its surroundings.

"Lo importante no es lo que se ve, sino lo que sucede entre las personas."
— Rirkrit Tiravanija

We understand art as a tool for understanding and reformulating our visual and conceptual language, as well as our relationship with time. Just as Primavera Sound has established itself as one of the most relevant music festivals of our era, we propose inviting artists whose practices reflect this cultural relevance — creating a space where the audience can perceive themselves and relate to one another in a different way, through experience, knowledge, and perception.

Through the use of natural materials, the project proposes a radical gesture of extraordinary impact: a place where people can pause, feel, and experience possible futures from the present.

A space conceived to breathe deeply and inhabit time from another dimension — a positive act of renewal that invites us to slow down in order to keep going. An experience that opens the possibility of reconnecting with oneself, with others, and with one's surroundings.

Two metabolisms04

Nothing is designed
to be discarded.

Green Cycle

Organic matter returns to earth.

Biomaterials designed to degrade with dignity and re-enter the biological cycle. What protects today can nourish tomorrow.

Blue Cycle

Every element retains value.

The structure is modular, demountable, and traceable. Each element can live multiple lives in the technosphere.

Inside the festival05

Rest inside change.

A climate refuge and sensory shelter within the intensity of Primavera Sound. Through filtered light, tactile biomaterials, and spaces for play and contemplation.

Comfort understood as something collective, emotional, and ecological. A slow rhythm inside the accelerated environment of the festival.

Sustainability protocol07

The framework
behind the shelter.

The Ethics of Impermanence

Architecture is a process, not a product. Nature teaches us that everything in an ecosystem has a certain duration — and that the exchange of energy and matter with the environment is crucial for its development.

Inspired by the Japanese Metabolism movement of the 1950–60s, this project questions time, not only space.

"Yes, perfect as a constant process of change. The impermanent beauty, the immaterial beauty."— Kisho Kurokawa

Material Lifespan

  • Appliances5–10 years
  • Interior design5–20 years
  • Services / MEP5–30 years
  • Skin / envelope30–60 years
  • Structure60–600 years

Biosphere Nutrients

The Green Cycle allows organic matter to return safely to the earth through composting and natural degradation. Materials in this cycle are designed to re-enter nature without contaminating it.

The shelter's biomaterial skins — mussel shells, eggshells, coffee grounds, orange peels — are all Green Cycle materials.

Material Types

  • BiodegradableReturns 100% naturally to the organic cycle through biological activity.
  • BioregenerativeSelf-sustaining and balanced. Each element sustains and is sustained by the others.
  • Bio-enhancerMaterial that re-enters nature and simultaneously improves its environment.
  • BiocompostableMust disintegrate 100% within two months of entering the composting process.

Technosphere Nutrients

The Blue Cycle ensures that structural elements remain modular, traceable, and reusable over time. Technical materials don't die — they circulate indefinitely in the technosphere.

Each time a material is reused, its embodied energy is reduced by up to 50%.

Strategies

  • Primary ReuseProduct reused in its original form and purpose.
  • Secondary ReuseReuse with a different purpose. Requires minimal modification.
  • UpcyclingTransforms waste into something of equal or greater value.
  • No DowncyclingAvoid processes that reduce material quality — design for reuse from the start.
01

Method

  • Document the deconstruction process
  • List all materials and connections
  • Design an accessible disassembly strategy
  • Define the future reuse of each component
02

Materials

  • Select by environmental impact
  • Abolish composite / hybrid materials
  • Differentiate Green and Blue cycle parts
  • Eliminate toxic or high-carbon materials
03

Connections

  • Mechanical joints only
  • No glue, no sealants
  • Accessible connections, minimal tools
  • Standard connectors for future reuse
04

Standardisation

  • Build on a grid, use modules
  • Modularity aids workers and reuse
  • Use standard systems and connections
  • Non-standard → natural cycle

No Waste, Only Nutrients

Eliminating the concept of waste means designing from the beginning with the understanding that there is no waste — only nutrients. The nutrients themselves determine the design.

"Building is a natural function. All constructive activity has as a framework nature."— Ken Yeang

Two Metabolisms

  • Green NutrientsAir, water, renewables, biosynthetics → return to biosphere
  • Blue NutrientsMetals, minerals, lab synthetics → circulate in technosphere
  • No HybridsComposite materials cannot be used in either cycle
  • Processes, Not ObjectsDesign is a metabolic process of continuous transformation
